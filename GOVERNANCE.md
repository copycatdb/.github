## ğŸ—ï¸ Decision Making

### Current Structure

- **Benevolent Dictator For Now (BDFN):** tinkeringforfun â€” final call on direction, architecture, and releases
- **Community input:** Issues, Discussions, and PRs are where decisions get shaped. The BDFN listens. Seriously.
- **Consensus preferred:** For anything significant, we aim for rough consensus. The dictator part is a tiebreaker, not a management style.

### How Decisions Get Made

1. **Small stuff** (bug fixes, minor features): Just open a PR. If it's good, it gets merged.
2. **Medium stuff** (new features, API changes): Open a Discussion first. Let the community weigh in. Then PR.
3. **Big stuff** (new repos, architectural changes, governance changes): Discussion + explicit sign-off from the BDFN + reasonable community feedback period.

This is inspired by how projects like curl and SQLite operate: a clear maintainer with strong community engagement, not design-by-committee, not corporate-controlled.

## ğŸ¤ What We Want From Corporations

We're not anti-corporate. We're pro-independence. There's a difference.

### What We'd Love to See (from Microsoft or anyone)

- **Use CopyCat tools.** That's why we built them.
- **Contribute code.** PRs from corporate developers are welcome and reviewed like any other.
- **Adopt our ideas.** If the RowWriter trait pattern from tabby inspires something in an official driver, that's a win for everyone. Take it. Seriously.
- **File issues.** You probably have use cases we haven't thought of.
- **Sponsor development.** GitHub Sponsors, grants, bounties â€” financial support is welcome and appreciated.
- **Tell people about us.** Blog posts, conference talks, internal recommendations.
- **Improve your own tools.** Competition makes everyone better. If CopyCat inspires Microsoft to improve their official tooling, the SQL Server community wins.

### What We Don't Want

- **Acquisition offers.** We're not for sale. Not now, not ever. Don't ask.
- **Embrace, extend, extinguish.** We know the history. We've read the memos. We're not naive, and we're not worried â€” because we're MIT licensed and community-owned. Fork us if you want. You can't kill what you don't control.
- **"Partnership" that means control.** If a partnership doesn't work with the Independence Clause above, it's not a partnership we want.
- **Pressure to change direction** to suit corporate roadmaps. We set our own priorities based on what the community needs.

We're building this in the open because we believe SQL Server tooling should be a community effort, not a corporate monopoly. Come build with us. Just don't try to buy the building.
